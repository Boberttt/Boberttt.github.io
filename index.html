Beamers, Beamers, and More Beamers

What is a Beamer?

Beamers are unskilled individuals who usually use pre-made malware to infect victims. Since these people are not skilled, they usually use stealers that only run, get data, send it to a command and control (C2) server, and then exit.

The Story (November 2022)

I was on Discord looking at some servers when I saw a message that caught my eye: "if anyone wants to learn how to hack, DM me". I thought the message was suspicious, so I decided to send a message to the person to see what they were up to. They asked if I wanted a stealer builder, and when they sent the download link, I didn't waste my time doing static analysis. Instead, I uploaded the "builder" to bazaar.abuse.ch (a mass sandboxing website) and waited. After a few hours, I looked at some of the sandboxes and saw that the network traffic was very revealing. I was able to see the Discord webhook used by the malware to deliver stolen data. I started spamming the webhook with messages like "unpacked by coffee&shells".

After the beamer realized that I had access to their webhook, they invited me to join their server (I don't know why). The server was about beaming other people using the owner's payloads. The way it worked was that when someone ran the payload, the whole server was able to see the dumped credentials via the Discord webhook. The server had about 20 members, mostly consisting of people who had been beamed (again, I don't know why). I used the webhook to delete legitimate logs (there were only two). I also constantly reported the download links, servers, and users.

This scared the owner (the beamer) so much that they deleted the server and made everyone move to a group chat, halving their member count!

At this point, I was getting bored of slowing down their operations, so I told the owner (the beamer) that I had reported them. Their response was: "Do you even know my main?" I didn't. In response, I told them that Discord would track their cookies and ban all of their accounts (this isn't true; I just told them this to scare them), and then they blocked me.

Thankfully, dioismymax, another hacker, gave me DAX's main username, DAX#6666! Feel free to report them! In conclusion, script kiddies are becoming more and more widespread to the point that they're dangerous to the general public (aka internet users).

How do I not get hacked?

Don't be dumb, don't click random links that strangers or friends send you. And have a decent anti-virus, like Bitdefender.
